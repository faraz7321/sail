# Roadmap & Next Steps

**Project:** SAIL · **Doc:** 14 · **Date:** 2026-07-18 · **Status:** Draft v1.0

This is our roadmap: a recap of the plan, how far we build, why we sequence it this way, and what we do next. Detail sits in [Business Model & Unit Economics](15_Business_Model_and_Unit_Economics.md), [Build Cost & Capital Plan](11_Build_Cost_and_Commercials.md), [Delivery Plan & Timeline](12_Delivery_Plan_and_Timeline.md), and [Risks](13_Risks_Assumptions_Dependencies.md).

## 1. Where we are

SAIL is a multi-tenant SaaS platform that turns US SMBs' own data plus external signals into dashboards, forecasts, and prescriptive AI recommendations — with daily cron jobs and per-tenant ICP personalization, aimed at cafés, restaurants, ice-cream shops, hotels, and motels. Planning is complete and we are pre-build: the architecture, phased plan, and economics are worked out, and our immediate job is to validate pricing with real target businesses and decide how we resource the build. From there we start Phase 0 and put a live MVP in pilot hands.

---

## 3. Why this sequence

- **De-risk before we scale spend.** The MVP proves the concept and validates pricing before we commit to the larger Phase 2–3 build.
- **Value early, not big-bang.** A live MVP in pilot hands means we learn from real usage instead of at the finish line.
- **A moat, not a report.** Prescriptive AI grounded in each tenant's own data and ICP is the recommendation layer competitors can't copy from a spec sheet — and it's what v1 adds.
- **Same low-commitment start.** Every path begins with Phase 0, so we can start now and pick the endpoint at the first gate once the numbers are real.

---

## 4. Immediate next steps for us

1. **Validate pricing** ($200 / $500 / $800, optional Lite $79–99) with **5–10 target businesses** — the founder leads.
2. **Choose the beachhead** — Square-first connector + café/QSR as the first vertical — both founders.
3. **Decide the build path** — in-house (developer-led) vs. contractor help for data eng / ML / QA — both founders.
4. **Decide budget & schedule** — set the build budget, operating run-rate, and delivery schedule, and line up funding to cover them — both founders.
5. **Start Phase 0** (Discovery & Design) — lock scope, architecture, and confirmed phase budgets.

---

## 5. Success criteria — the first stretch

Setup milestones:

- 5–10 pricing conversations completed with target businesses, with at least a handful of verbal commitments at target tiers.
- Square-first connector feasibility confirmed and the café/QSR beachhead chosen.
- Build path decided (in-house vs. contractor mix), with budget, schedule, and funding decided.
- Phase 0 complete: architecture locked, backlog seeded, and phase budgets confirmed at the discovery gate.

How we'll know it's working once the MVP is live:

- Paying pilots activated, with healthy activation and low early churn (retention holding).
- MRR trending up as pilots convert.
- Forecast accuracy holding against pilot data.
- Gross margin trending toward its target (~90%+ at scale) — see [Business Model & Unit Economics](15_Business_Model_and_Unit_Economics.md).
